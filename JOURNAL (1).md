# 6/25/2026 9 AM - More CAD

_Time spent: 2.1h_

I made the case top, changed screw size from M2.5 to M3, since it was hard finding long M2.5 screws for cheap, but I founr cheap M3 scerws, so I changed my hole size to 3.4mm diameter accounting for the tolerances. I also took measurements of my bicycle bar and the middle bar (where I aim to mount the battery) and made the mount. For my bar mount, I first made 2 semicircles with 3mm difference in their radii, and then I used the line tool to draw a line 5mm from the edge of the larger semi-circle and used the line tool to draw another line from the previous line end with an angle of 150 degrees, and I choose to end the line wherever the 150 degree line met the larger circle. I then extruded it with my desired bar length (70mm) and added the M3 holes in it.

# 5/18/2026 1 PM - Sensor selection and thorough planning

_Time spent: 4h_

I decided to make this for my bicycle, as a speedometer, accelerometer and a gyroscope. For the gyroscope and accelerometer, I found a good candidate, the LSM6DS3TR-C IMU, which does both. I then chose a GNSS module for my speedometer, which  is the NEO-M9N-00B with a ceramic patch antenna (ANT‑GNCP‑TH25AL12) with it. I will display all this information on my OLED. 

Communication protocols:
I2C- for the display
UART- for the NEO-M9N-00B
SPI (4-wire) - for the LSM6DS3TR-C 
The XIAO RP-2040 has all these communication protocols.
(Note- I checked the datasheets of all these components to make sure they support their respective communication protocols.)
(One more thing- I also checked LCSC to make sure they have the stock for all these sensors :D )
![image](https://stasis.hackclub-assets.com/images/1779111132370-aw44t4.png)
